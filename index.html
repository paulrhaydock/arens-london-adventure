🇬🇧 FRIDAY 🌧️

Day 1 - We arrive in London!
🌧️ Rainy today - but everything we do is inside!
✈️
10:00

We land at the airport!

We fly into Stansted Airport. Then we get on a fast train called the Stansted Express. It takes us right into London!

🚆
11:00

First Underground ride!

We arrive at Liverpool Street and go down into the London Underground for the very first time! We ride the Circle Line for one stop.

🌉 Tower Bridge
🌉
11:30

Tower Bridge!

We walk on a glass floor high up above the River Thames! You can look down and see boats below your feet. It is 42 metres up in the air! Alessia has a special pass to get us in.

💡 Fun fact: Tower Bridge is NOT London Bridge! They are two different bridges. Tower Bridge is the one that opens up to let big ships through.
🍔
12:30

Lunch time!

Time to eat! Leesa is coming to meet us too.

🚇
1:40

Circle Line to Farringdon

A nice long Tube ride - 12 minutes! Time to sit down and rest.

📨 Mail Rail
📨
2:00

Mail Rail - Secret Underground Train!

We go deep under the ground to ride a tiny train through secret tunnels! These tunnels used to carry letters and parcels under London. The train is really small and goes through dark tunnels with lights. It is 21 metres under the ground!

💡 Fun fact: Mail Rail carried 4 million letters every day! The trains ran without any drivers.
⚡
3:30

Platform 9 and 3/4!

We go to King's Cross Station - the same station Harry Potter uses! There is a trolley that goes right into the wall. You can push it and get your photo taken! They give you a magic scarf and a wand. Then we can look in the Harry Potter Shop - it has Chocolate Frogs and wands!

💡 Fun fact: Look at the building next door - St Pancras Station. It looks like a castle! In the Harry Potter films, they used THIS building as the outside of King's Cross.
⚡ Platform 9 3/4
💡
4:30

Piccadilly Circus!

We ride the Piccadilly Line to see the giant bright screens at Piccadilly Circus! There are huge TVs everywhere and a statue called Eros. It is SO bright and busy!

🏠
5:00

Home time!

We take the Northern Line all the way to Balham where Auntie Leesa lives. Dinner, bath, stories, bed! Big day tomorrow!

🏴󠁧󠁢󠁥󠁮󠁧󠁿 SATURDAY ☀️

Day 2 - The Big Day! Sunny and amazing!
☀️ Sunny today! The best day for seeing things outside. Wrap up warm though - only 3 to 11 degrees!
🚇
8:30

Northern Line to Westminster

22 minutes on the Tube to start our big day!

🕒 Big Ben
👑 Buckingham Palace
🕒
9:00

🇬🇧 Big Ben!

The most famous clock in the whole world! It is 96 metres tall. We can hear it go BONG! We walk all around it and take photos.

💡 Fun fact: Big Ben is not the name of the tower. It is the name of the big bell inside! The tower is called the Elizabeth Tower.
🏞️
9:30

Walk through St James's Park

A really nice park with a big lake. Look for the pelicans! Yes, real pelicans live in this park in London! And lots of ducks too.

👑
10:00

🇬🇧 Buckingham Palace!

This is where the King lives! 👑 It has a huge gold gate and a big statue in front.

💂
11:00

🏴󠁧󠁢󠁥󠁮󠁧󠁿 Changing of the Guard!

Soldiers in red coats and big fluffy hats march right past us with a band playing music! They swap over who is looking after the King. We watch from The Mall - the big red road that leads to the Palace.

💡 Fun fact: The guards' big fluffy hats are made from real bear fur. Each one weighs almost half a kilo!
🦁
12:00

Trafalgar Square!

A huge square with a really tall column and four giant lion statues. You can climb on the lions! There are big fountains too.

🦁 Trafalgar Square
🍕
12:30

Lunch!

Yum yum! Eating near Trafalgar Square.

🚍
1:25

🇬🇧 Red Double-Decker Bus!

We climb to the top deck of a big red London bus! We sit right at the front and see London from up high. The bus goes past famous buildings and over a bridge. So cool!

🎡
2:00

The London Eye!

The biggest wheel in London! It is 135 metres tall. We get to see it up close and walk around it. Maybe we even go on it!

🎡 London Eye
🚕
2:20

🇬🇧 Black Cab Ride!

We get in a real London black taxi! It takes us across a big bridge over the River Thames and through the middle of London to see St Paul's. The inside of a black cab is really cool - the seats face each other!

⛪
2:30

St Paul's Cathedral!

A huge church with an amazing round dome on top. It is one of the biggest churches in the world!

🪄
2:45

The Harry Potter Bridge!

This is the Millennium Bridge. In Harry Potter, the Death Eaters blow it up! We walk across it with St Paul's behind us and can see the River Thames below.

🪄 Millennium Bridge + St Paul's
🚂
3:15

DLR - You're the Driver!

This is the best bit! The DLR train has NO DRIVER. It drives itself! You sit at the very front and look out the big window. It feels like YOU are driving the train through London! We ride it out to Canary Wharf and back.

💡 Fun fact: DLR stands for Docklands Light Railway. The trains are totally automatic - there really is no driver at all!
🏠
3:50

Home time!

Northern Line back to Balham. What a day! Red bus, black cab, DLR, AND the Tube. Time to rest!

🇬🇧 SUNDAY 🚂

Day 3 - The Flying Scotsman!
☁️ Cloudy today, about 4 to 11 degrees. Dress warm for the train stations!
🌟 Today we go on the most famous train in the WHOLE WORLD! 🌟
🚂 The Flying Scotsman!
🚇
8:15

Tube to Waterloo

Northern Line to Waterloo Station - one of the biggest stations in London!

🚆
8:45

Train to Alton

We take a South Western Railway train from Waterloo. It goes for one hour through the countryside! Look out for fields, trees, and villages.

🚂
10:00 - 2:30

THE FLYING SCOTSMAN!

This is it! The most famous steam train in the whole world! It is nearly 100 years old and still works! We ride in carriages pulled by the real Flying Scotsman through the Hampshire countryside for 20 miles. The train goes choo-choo and puffs steam into the sky! You can smell the steam and coal. There are also tiny trains we can ride at the stations.

💡 Fun fact: The Flying Scotsman was the first steam train to go 100 miles per hour! It was built in 1923 - that was over 100 years ago. It is painted green and has the number 4472.
🌭

Food on the train!

There are places to get food and drinks at the stations along the way. We can eat while we watch the steam train!

😴
3:00

Train back to London

Back on the South Western Railway to London. You might have a little nap! Then a quick train to Balham.

🏠
4:10

Back at Leesa's!

What an amazing day! Dinner, stories about the steam train, then bed.

🏴󠁧󠁢󠁥󠁮󠁧󠁿 MONDAY 🏁

Day 4 - Last morning, then home!
☁️ Cloudy, 4-11 degrees. Our last London morning!
🚌
9:30

London Transport Museum!

This museum is all about buses and trains! There are old red buses you can climb on, old Underground trains you can go inside, and a play area called "All Aboard" where you can pretend to drive a bus! There is even a real steam train from 1866 - that is really, really old!

🚈
12:00

Elizabeth Line!

A quick ride on the brand new Elizabeth Line. The stations are AMAZING - they look like they are from the future! Big, shiny, and super cool.

🍔
12:30

Last London lunch!

One more yummy lunch, then back to Leesa's to get our bags.

🚆
2:15

Thameslink to Gatwick Airport!

Our very last train ride! Thameslink from Balham all the way to Gatwick Airport. 35 minutes - one last ride to enjoy!

✈️
5:20

Fly home!

Bye bye London! What an amazing adventure!
